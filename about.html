About

This webpage aims to give all active members of our campus the ability to choose a commuting option that is best for their individual needs, after weighing the real cost of each transportation mode. Single occupant vehicle, multi-occupant vehicle, motorcycle, regional transit commute sleeve, bike, walk, and rideShare services such as uber/lyft are the seven commuting modes that users will be able to select from. All commuting modes will consider the destination start time, and monetary costs to be factors of each mode's real cost. For the first three modes, this webpage will consider: cost of the vehicle itself, maintenance cost of vehicle, insurance costs, parking permit costs, carbon emissions, traffic contributions as factors of their real cost. Regional transit commutes accessible through a OneCard will consider: trip planner, carbon emissions, and traffic contribution as factors of its real cost. Bike and walking will consider the same costs as the regional transit commutes, except for carbon emissions and traffic contribution. RideShare services such as Uber/Lyft will consider the traffic contribution and encroachment on student parking spaces as factors of its real cost.
